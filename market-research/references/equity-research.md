# Equity And ADR Research

Use this reference for common stocks and ADRs.

Required sections:

1. Executive summary: facts first, then interpretation.
2. Company overview: business model, segments, geography, customer/end-market exposure.
3. Source base: latest annual filing, latest quarterly filing if available, investor presentation or earnings release if used.
4. Financial profile: revenue, margins, net income, cash flow, balance sheet, share count, and capital returns when available.
5. Strategy and moat: separate management statements from your interpretation.
6. Valuation context: multiples, cash-flow context, or peer comparison only when source support is available.
7. Risks: business, financial, regulatory, cyclicality, customer concentration, FX, and ADR-specific risks where relevant.
8. Data gaps: unavailable, stale, or low-confidence facts and what public source was attempted.

Primary sources:

- Latest 10-K, 20-F, 40-F, 10-Q, or 6-K as applicable.
- SEC companyfacts for deterministic metric extraction when available.
- Company investor-relations releases and presentations.
- Exchange/company profile pages for identity checks when filings are unavailable.

Do not use consensus estimates, analyst ratings, or proprietary data unless the user supplies a local export. Public analyst commentary may be summarized only as secondary context.
