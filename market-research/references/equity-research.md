# Equity And ADR Research

Use this reference for common stocks and ADRs.

Required sections:

1. Executive summary: facts first, then interpretation.
2. Market snapshot and positioning: current or recent price, 52-week range, market capitalization, volume, short interest, basic moving averages, and visible sentiment/analyst context when free/public data is available.
3. Company overview: business model, segments, geography, customer/end-market exposure, management, and how the company actually makes money.
4. Source base: latest annual filing, latest quarterly filing if available, earnings release, investor presentation, proxy, insider filing, or market-data source if used.
5. Financial profile: revenue, margins, net income, EPS, cash flow, balance sheet, share count, capital returns, leverage, liquidity, and working-capital trends when available.
6. SEC filing review: latest 10-K/20-F/40-F risk factors and MD&A, latest 10-Q/6-K changes, recent 8-Ks, proxy governance/compensation, Form 4 insider activity, and a recent-filings sweep from SEC submissions. Mention or explicitly dismiss recent filings that are not thesis-relevant, such as board appointments, Form SD, shelf filings, or administrative disclosures.
7. Sector, industry, and moat: industry structure, competitors, relative growth/margins, regulation, pricing power, and moat assessment. Separate management statements from interpretation.
8. Tariff, regulatory, and macro exposure: map known revenue, COGS, supply-chain, FX, rate, commodity, and policy sensitivities. Quantify only when source support exists.
9. Valuation context: trailing and forward-looking multiples only when sourced, cash-flow context, peer comparison, simple scenario ranges, reverse-DCF style expectations, and dividend/buyback support where applicable.
10. Catalysts and trader decision framework: next earnings date if available, product/regulatory/company events, bull/base/bear setup, Buy/Hold/Sell/Avoid-style view, conviction, key monitor list, and invalidation triggers.
11. Risks: business, financial, regulatory, cyclicality, customer/supplier concentration, competition, valuation, FX, geopolitical, litigation, cybersecurity, governance, strategic-partner/platform dependency, and ADR-specific risks where relevant. For AI-heavy companies, explicitly address AI infrastructure spend, monetization, model/provider/platform dependency, partner economics, and regulatory risk when supported by public sources.
12. Data gaps: unavailable, stale, or low-confidence facts and what public source was attempted.

Primary sources:

- Latest 10-K, 20-F, 40-F, 10-Q, or 6-K as applicable.
- SEC companyfacts for deterministic metric extraction when available.
- Company investor-relations releases and presentations.
- Proxy, Form 4, and recent 8-K filings when material to governance, compensation, insider activity, M&A, restatements, or guidance.
- Public/free market data such as exchange pages, Nasdaq/NYSE pages, Stooq, Yahoo Finance pages/downloads, company IR quote pages, or other no-login sources. Record these as secondary unless they are exchange/issuer primary data.
- Exchange/company profile pages for identity checks when filings are unavailable.

Do not require paid subscriptions or API keys. Consensus estimates, analyst ratings, options flow, unusual options activity, gamma positioning, TAM estimates, and transcript tone are useful only when accessible from public/free sources or user-supplied exports. If unavailable or not reproducible, mark them `Data not available` or `unverified` and do not weaken the report with invented precision.

Completion and financing language:

- Use `closed`, `completed`, `settled`, `converted`, `redeemed`, or similar status words only when a direct closing/completion source is frozen or user-supplied.
- If only an announcement, prospectus, or expected timetable is frozen, use `announced`, `planned`, `expected`, or `pending` and list the closing source as a data gap.

Market-data calculations:

- Label adjusted-close calculations explicitly.
- Keep source dates separate from fiscal periods and filing dates for SEC-derived facts.
